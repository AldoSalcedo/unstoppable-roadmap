## SALARY RESEARCH TOOLS

### Compensation Data Sites
- **Levels.fyi** (levels.fyi)
  - Use: See exact salary data by company, role, level, location

## FREELANCE PLATFORMS

### Managed Platforms (They Screen Clients)
- **Toptal** (toptal.com)
  - Use: Pre-vetted, high-quality clients ($80-120/hr typical)
  - Process: Apply, interview, get certified, get access to jobs
  - Pro tip: Higher barrier to entry = better clients
  - Timeline: 2-4 weeks to get approved

- **Gun.io** (gun.io)
  - Use: High-end freelance talent for startups
  - Specialization: Full-stack, React, Node, AWS
  - Pro tip: Better clients than Upwork, less supply
  - Timeline: Similar vetting process

### Self-Service Platforms (You Manage Clients)
- **Upwork** (upwork.com)
  - Use: Find clients, submit proposals, get jobs
  - Rate: $75-100/hr (but take 20% for platform)
  - Strategy:
    - Create profile with portfolio link
    - Specialize: "React/Next.js for Healthcare Tech"
    - Write cover letter for each proposal (not template)
    - Target: 50 proposals/week for first month
  - Pro tip: First few jobs are hard; once you have reviews, easier
  - Caveat: Lots of low-quality clients, need to filter

- **Fiverr** (fiverr.com)
  - Use: Micro-services, short projects
  - Rate: Variable (typically lower than Upwork)
  - Strategy: Not recommended for your level (more junior-focused)

### Niche Platforms
- **We Work Remotely Jobs** (weworkremotely.com)
  - Use: Post as freelancer, get clients
  - Rate: $75-120/hr
  - Pro tip: Smaller platform, less competitive

- **Fancy Hands** (fancyhands.com)
  - Use: Task-based freelance
  - Rate: Lower (not ideal for SWE)
  - Caveat: Skip this

### Direct Outreach (Best ROI)
- **LinkedIn Direct Outreach**
  - Target: CTOs, Founders, Engineering Managers at startups
  - Message: "I'm available for contract work on [tech]. Portfolio: [link]"
  - Expected rate: $85-120/hour (higher than platforms)
  - Expected close rate: 5-10% (but higher quality clients)

- **Company websites**
  - Look for: "Work with us" / "Hire us" page
  - Email: contact@company.com or partnerships@company.com
  - Message: Custom pitch (see sprint-week15-16.md for template)

## INTERVIEW PREP RESOURCES

### LeetCode (Live Coding Practice)
- **Website**: leetcode.com
- **Strategy**:
  - Subscribe: Premium ($159/year) OR free tier is OK
  - Focus: Medium difficulty (not hard)
  - Problems: Array, String, Tree, Graph, DP, Sliding Window
  - Practice: 5-7 problems/day for 2 weeks
  - Time: 45-60 min per problem (simulate interview)
  - Whiteboard: Write pseudocode first, then code
- **Healthcare-relevant patterns**:
  - Data validation (HIPAA, PII)
  - Caching (patient records)
  - Real-time updates (notifications)
  - Search + filtering (medical records)

### Interview.io (Mock Interviews)
- **Website**: interview.io
- **Use**: Schedule mock interview with real engineers
- **Price**: $99-299 per mock (or free tier with feedback)
- **Value**: Real-time feedback from people doing the job
- **Pro tip**: Do 2-3 mocks before final rounds

### System Design (For Final Rounds)
- **Resources**:
  - Grokking System Design Interview (educative.io)
  - System Design Interview (alex-xu.com)
  - YouTube: "System Design Interview" channels (Hussein Nasser, etc.)
- **Healthcare scenarios to practice**:
  - Design an appointment booking system (Zocdoc-style)
  - Design a patient notification system (real-time, scalable)
  - Design a lab results platform (Benchling-style)
  - Design a telehealth video system (Ro-style)
- **Structure**:
  - Requirements → Scale → Components → Deep dive → Tradeoffs

### Behavioral Interview (STAR Method)
- **STAR**: Situation, Task, Action, Result
- **Practice questions**:
  - "Tell me about a conflict with a coworker"
  - "Tell me about a time you failed"
  - "Tell me about your proudest achievement"
  - "Tell me about a hard technical problem"
- **Video practice**: Record yourself answering, watch playback
- **Books**: "Cracking the Coding Interview" (6th edition)

### Company-Specific Deep Dives
- **For each interview**:
  - Read: Recent company blog posts (2-3)
  - Watch: Company mission video (5 min)
  - Know: 2-3 specific features/products
  - Understand: Recent news/funding/growth
  - Prepare: "Why this company?" (personalized, not generic)

## NEGOTIATION & OFFER RESOURCES

### Negotiation Frameworks
- **BATNA** (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement)
  - Know your minimum (walk-away number)
  - Know your target (ideal salary)
  - Know your stretch (reach goal)
  - Example: Min=$70k, Target=$90k, Stretch=$110k

- **Anchoring** (Power of first number)
  - Let them anchor first (they'll go low)
  - Counter-offer 10-15% higher than their ask
  - Use market data (Levels.fyi, Glassdoor) to justify
  - Move slowly (don't accept immediately)

### Compensation Data for Negotiation
- **Levels.fyi**: See exact salary by company, level, location
- **Blind**: Anonymous salary discussions

- **PayScale**: General benchmarking
- **Your angle**: Biology background + auditing + bilingual = premium positioning

## TECHNICAL DEEP DIVES

### Healthcare Tech Knowledge
- **HIPAA Compliance**:
  - Resource: HHS guide (hhs.gov/hipaa)
  - Key: Understand data encryption, access logs, audit trails
  - Impact on engineering: Security-first design

- **Healthcare Data Types**:
  - PHI (Protected Health Information): SSN, medical record number, etc.
  - Handling: Must be encrypted, access controlled, logged
  - Engineering: Privacy by design

- **Interoperability Standards**:
  - HL7: Healthcare messaging format (old, complex)
  - FHIR: Modern API standard for healthcare data (JSON-based, RESTful)
  - Resource: hl7.org/fhir (read "Introduction")
  - Impact: Understanding how healthcare systems talk to each other

- **Telemedicine Tech**:
  - Video: WebRTC (real-time communication)
  - Security: End-to-end encryption
  - Scalability: CDN, load balancing for video
  - Examples: Ro, Teladoc, Doctor on Demand

### Relevant AWS Services
- **EC2** (virtual servers): Compute
- **RDS** (relational database): HIPAA-compliant DB
- **S3** (storage): HIPAA-compliant file storage
- **Lambda** (serverless): Event-driven architecture
- **API Gateway**: REST API management
- **VPC** (virtual private cloud): Network isolation
- **IAM** (identity access): Who can do what
- **CloudTrail**: Audit logs (HIPAA requirement)

### Azure AI Services (Relevant to your cert)
- **Azure Cognitive Services**: ML APIs (vision, language, etc.)
- **Healthcare NLP**: De-identification, medical understanding
- **Use case**: Extract medical entities from patient notes
- **Relevance to healthcare startups**: AI for medical insights
