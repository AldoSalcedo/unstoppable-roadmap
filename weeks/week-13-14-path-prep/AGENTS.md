# 🤖 AGENTS: Week 13-14 Context

This document describes how to work with AI agents (Claude, ChatGPT, etc.) during this 2-week intensive block.

---

## Agent Role in Week 13-14

Your agents are **thinking partners and preparation coaches**, not replacements for real mentors. Use them for:

- **Interview prep:** Mock interviewer, feedback on answers
- **System design:** Whiteboard partner, challenge your assumptions
- **Content creation:** Brainstorming, editing, structure
- **Networking:** Message refinement, strategy testing
- **Path analysis:** Pro/con lists, decision frameworks
- **Learning:** Explain complex concepts (HIPAA, management, SaaS metrics)

---

## How to Use Agents Effectively

### 1. Mock Interview Partner

**Setup:**
```
"You're my senior technical interviewer for a healthcare engineer role.
I'm interviewing for [company/position]. Please conduct a mock interview
covering behavioral questions, system design, and technical depth.

For each answer, give me immediate feedback on:
- Did I answer the question directly?
- What could I improve?
- What was strong?

Let's start with a behavioral question."
```

**Best practices:**
- Do 2-3 mock interviews with an agent per week
- Record yourself or take notes on feedback
- Iterate on weak answers and re-do the interview

### 2. System Design Partner

**Setup:**
```
"I'm preparing to interview at [company] for a system design round.
Let's design [system: EMR, telehealth, prescription management].

Requirements:
- 100k concurrent users
- HIPAA compliant
- 99.95% uptime

I'll walk you through my design, and you ask clarifying questions,
challenge my assumptions, and point out gaps."
```

**Best practices:**
- Draw on paper/whiteboard, then describe to agent
- Agent plays the interviewer (asks hard questions)
- Ask: "What's the biggest risk in my design?"
- Iterate on feedback

### 3. Content Creation Partner

**Setup:**
```
"I'm writing LinkedIn posts about [topic: HIPAA compliance, team leadership, SaaS validation].

Here's my outline:
[paste your outline]

Can you:
1. Suggest 3 alternative angles
2. Improve the hook
3. Identify gaps in my argument
4. Suggest a call-to-action
5. Review for clarity (write at 8th grade level)"
```

**Best practices:**
- Write your own first draft (don't ask agent to write)
- Agent helps refine, not create
- Test posts on real audience for feedback

### 4. Networking Message Coach

**Setup:**
```
"I'm reaching out to [person's name] at [company] on LinkedIn.
Here's my message:

[paste your draft]

Feedback needed:
1. Is it personalized enough (does it feel generic)?
2. Is my ask clear and not too big?
3. Would YOU respond to this?
4. What's missing?
5. Any tone issues?"
```

**Best practices:**
- Write 3-5 versions, test with agent
- Agent plays the recipient (would they respond?)
- Keep messages under 100 words

### 5. Path Decision Partner

**Setup:**
```
"I'm choosing between three paths:

PATH 1: SPECIALIST (Healthcare engineer consulting)
Pros: [list]
Cons: [list]
Excitement level: 7/10

PATH 2: LEADER (Engineering leadership)
Pros: [list]
Cons: [list]
Excitement level: 6/10

PATH 3: BUILDER (SaaS founder)
Pros: [list]
Cons: [list]
Excitement level: 8/10

Help me think through which is the best fit for my:
- Values (what matters to me)
- Skills (where I'm strong)
- Financial needs (income requirements)
- Lifestyle (how I want to work)"
```

**Best practices:**
- Do this analysis yourself first
- Agent helps you think through trade-offs
- Ask: "What am I not considering?"
- Sleep on the decision, revisit next day

## Agent Prompts for Specific Tasks

### Prompt: "Help me prepare for [specific role] interview"

```
I'm interviewing for a [Senior Engineer / Engineering Manager / Founder Track]
position at [Company].

Context:
- My background: [brief]
- My strengths: [3-4 areas]
- Areas I want to improve: [2-3 areas]
- Interview format: [behavioral / system design / technical / leadership]

Can you:
1. Generate 5 tough questions for this role
2. Help me craft strong answers (STAR format where relevant)
3. Identify any gaps or weak spots
4. Suggest follow-up questions I should ask them

Let's do a full mock interview."
```

### Prompt: "Help me refine my healthcare tech positioning"

```
I'm positioning myself as a healthcare software engineer. Here's my context:

Background:
- Biology/QBP degree (so I understand the domain)
- Auditing experience (compliance/process thinking)
- React/React Native skills
- AWS + Azure certifications
- 2-3 portfolio projects

I want to be known for:
- HIPAA-compliant system design
- Healthcare domain expertise
- Full-stack healthcare solutions

Questions:
1. How should I talk about my unique positioning?
2. Which healthcare companies should I target?
3. What specific expertise should I develop next?
4. How do I bridge biology background + engineering credibility?
```

### Prompt: "Help me define my SaaS idea"

```
I'm exploring a SaaS idea in the healthcare space.

Problem I'm solving: [describe in 1-2 sentences]

Target customers: [who specifically]

Current solution they use: [what they do today]

Why it's broken: [3-4 pain points]

My solution: [what you'd build]

Now help me:
1. Stress-test this problem (is it really that painful?)
2. Identify 5 companies I could talk to
3. Design a customer interview script
4. Define the MVP (what's the 80/20?)
5. Estimate initial market size
```

### Prompt: "Help me think about leadership readiness"

```
I'm considering a transition to engineering management.

My current situation:
- Years as IC: [X]
- Team size I've influenced: [X]
- My leadership style: [describe]
- Why I want to manage: [motivations]

My concerns:
- [Concern 1]
- [Concern 2]
- [Concern 3]

Help me think through:
1. Am I ready for this transition? What would make me more ready?
2. What are the biggest pitfalls I should avoid?
3. How do I keep technical skills sharp while managing?
4. What's a realistic timeline (6 months? 1 year?)
5. Who should I learn from?
```

## What Agents CAN'T Do (Limitations)

- **Real interviews:** Agent feedback is helpful, but real people matter more
- **Relationship building:** Agents can't replace mentors, coffee chats, real networking
- **Domain expertise:** For healthcare specifics, verify with domain experts
- **Current data:** Agent knowledge has cutoff date; verify recent changes
- **Personalized career advice:** Agent is a thinking partner, not your career coach
- **Hold you accountable:** YOU must set the deadline and hit it

---

## What Agents DO Well

- **Brainstorming:** Rapid ideation, multiple angles
- **Practice:** Safe environment to try answers
- **Clarity:** Explaining complex concepts
- **Feedback:** Immediate reaction to your ideas
- **Iteration:** Quick edits and refinement
- **Research:** Helping you structure investigation

## Agent Protocols

### When to Use an Agent

✓ You're stuck and need to brainstorm
✓ You want to practice before talking to humans
✓ You need feedback on writing/messaging
✓ You want to test ideas quickly
✓ You need to learn a concept fast

### When NOT to Use an Agent

✗ You need real mentorship (find a person)
✗ You're making a major decision without reflection (sleep on it)
✗ You're avoiding hard conversation (talk to the person directly)
✗ You want validation (this is an echo chamber)
✗ You need current market data (verify with real sources)

## Sample Conversation: How to Brief an Agent

```
YOU: "Hey, I'm doing Week 13 of my 12-week roadmap. I'm in the
'Three Paths Preparation' block. I need help thinking about which
path to choose."

AGENT: "Got it. Three paths: Specialist, Leader, or Builder. Tell
me about your context and what's pulling you toward each."

YOU: "I have a biology background and auditing experience. React/React
Native skills, AWS/Azure certs. I'm torn between:
- Path 1 (Specialist): Healthcare consulting, maybe $100-150/hr
- Path 2 (Leader): Management track toward VP
- Path 3 (Builder): Build my own SaaS in healthcare

What I love: autonomy, impact, learning. What I need: decent income,
some stability, fulfillment."

AGENT: "Strong context. Let's map this:
- Autonomy: Path 1 (highest), Path 3 (very high), Path 2 (moderate)
- Income security: Path 2 (highest), Path 1 (good), Path 3 (risky)
- Learning: Path 1 (deep domain), Path 2 (people), Path 3 (full stack)

What's your gut telling you RIGHT NOW?"

YOU: "Honestly, Path 3 excites me most, but it scares me because
the income is unpredictable."

AGENT: "That's valuable self-knowledge. So the question is: Can you
afford the risk? How many months of runway do you have? And is
the excitement worth the instability for you? Some people thrive
on that, others don't."

YOU: "I have 18 months of savings. And I think I'd regret not trying.
But I'm not ready to commit yet."

AGENT: "Perfect. So Week 13 task: DO MORE CUSTOMER INTERVIEWS
for your idea. Talk to 10 people with the problem. That'll either
confirm it's worth the risk or make you realize 'actually, this isn't
as important as I thought.' Let me help you design the interview script."

YOU: "Yes, that's helpful. Here's my first draft script..."
```

## Red Flags: When to Pause Agent Advice

- Agent is giving you "the answer" instead of helping you think
- You're using agent to avoid real conversations
- Agent's feedback contradicts multiple real people you trust
- You feel less confident after talking to agent (should feel more)
- Agent is substituting for actual learning/research

---

## Best Agent Practices (Checklist)

- [ ] **Be specific:** More context = better feedback
- [ ] **Iterate:** Don't expect perfection on draft 1
- [ ] **Verify:** Cross-check agent advice with real people
- [ ] **Reflect:** Sleep on agent feedback before acting
- [ ] **Document:** Save good feedback for later reference
- [ ] **Appreciate:** Thank the agent (it's good practice)
- [ ] **Accountability:** Use agent for accountability, not excuses

## Agent as Your "Thinking Partner"

Best way to use agents: As a thinking partner who asks good questions and helps you clarify YOUR thinking, not as someone who tells you what to do.

Good agent interaction:
```
YOU: "I'm not sure about Path 1."
AGENT: "What specifically concerns you about it?"
YOU: "I'm worried consulting will be isolating."
AGENT: "Tell me more about that. Have you consulted before?"
YOU: "No, but I imagine working alone all day."
AGENT: "Interesting. What if you worked with other consultants? Or
took on collaborative projects? What specifically appeals to you about
being part of a team in Path 2?"
```

Bad agent interaction:
```
YOU: "I'm torn between Path 1 and Path 2."
AGENT: "You should definitely choose Path 2. Here's why..."
```

## Summary

**Agents are most valuable as:**
- Interview practice partners (safe space to fail)
- Writing coaches (feedback on clarity, tone)
- Brainstorming partners (rapid ideation)
- Concept teachers (explain complex ideas)
- Thinking partners (help you clarify YOUR thoughts)

**Agents are NOT:**
- Your career mentor (find a person)
- Your decision maker (you decide)
- Your accountability buddy (find humans)
- Your only feedback (needs reality-testing)

---

**Use agents wisely. They accelerate your learning, but human relationships are irreplaceable.**
