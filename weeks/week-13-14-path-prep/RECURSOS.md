## 1. System Design & Technical Interview Prep

### Structured Learning

**Grokking System Design Interview** (Educative)
- Format: Interactive video + whiteboard exercises
- Cost: $40-80 (often on sale)
- Why it's good: Shows HOW to think, not just WHAT to know
- Time: 15-20 hours to complete
- URL: https://www.educative.io/courses/grokking-system-design-interview

**ByteByteGo by Alex Xu** (Excellent for scale)
- Format: Newsletter + courses
- Cost: Free newsletter, $50+ for premium
- Why it's good: Real-world systems (Netflix, Uber, etc.)
- Focus: Scale, caching, databases
- URL: https://bytebytego.com/

**Designing Data-Intensive Applications** (Book)
- Author: Martin Kleppmann
- Cost: $40-50
- Why it's good: Deep dive on databases, consistency, fault tolerance
- Time: 30-50 hours to read thoroughly
- Key chapters: 5 (consistency), 6 (partitioning), 7 (transactions)

### Practice Platforms

**LeetCode System Design** (Free tier available)
- Cost: Free with paid upgrades
- Format: Problems with discussion
- Why it's good: Real interview questions, community solutions
- URL: https://leetcode.com/discuss/interview-question/system-design

**Excalidraw** (Free whiteboard)
- Cost: Completely free
- Format: Online drawing tool
- Why it's good: Practice drawing architectures, share with mentors
- URL: https://excalidraw.com/

**Miro** (Collaborative whiteboard)
- Cost: Free tier available
- Format: Real-time collaboration
- Why it's good: Practice with others remotely
- URL: https://miro.com/

## 2. Healthcare-Specific Resources

### Understanding Healthcare Tech

**Epic Systems Overview** (Free from Epic)
- What: EHR (Electronic Health Record) system, industry standard
- Why: 50% of US hospitals use Epic
- Learn: Epic's architecture and how they solved scale/compliance
- URL: https://www.epic.com/ (explore "Technology" section)

**FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources)** (Free, official standard)
- What: Standard for healthcare data exchange
- Why: Modern healthcare APIs use FHIR
- Learn: HL7 FHIR specification
- URL: https://www.hl7.org/fhir/

**HIPAA for Developers** (Various free resources)
- CMS Official: https://www.hipaa.gov/ (official compliance guide)
- CloudFlare guide: https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/compliance/hipaa/
- AWS HIPAA overview: https://aws.amazon.com/compliance/hipaa/
- Why: Core compliance knowledge for healthcare engineers

**Teladoc, CVS Health, Athena** (Case studies)
- Research their engineering blogs
- Follow CTO/VPs on LinkedIn
- Read their SEC filings (if public) for tech strategy
- Look for engineering jobs (shows tech stack they use)

### Healthcare Domain Knowledge

**Coursera: Introduction to Healthcare IT** (Audit free)
- Cost: Free audit option
- Time: 4-6 hours
- Why: Basic healthcare terminology, EMR/EHR systems
- URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/healthcare-information-technology

**Stanford: Healthcare IT** (Free YouTube lectures)
- Cost: Free
- Format: Video lectures
- Why: University-level healthcare IT overview
- URL: Search "Stanford Healthcare IT" on YouTube

## 3. Mock Interview Partners

### Finding Interview Partners

**ADPList** (Free mentorship)
- What: Volunteer mentors (product, leadership, career)
- Cost: Free
- Format: 30-min video calls
- Why: Real mentors who've done interviews
- URL: https://adplist.org/

**LeetCode Discuss** (Community)
- What: Find people doing mock interviews
- Cost: Free
- Format: Connect in comments, schedule calls
- Why: Engineers prepping for same companies
- URL: https://leetcode.com/discuss/

**Pramp** (Free mock interviews)
- What: Peer-to-peer mock interview platform
- Cost: Free
- Format: Video interview with another person
- Why: Real human feedback, schedule flexibility
- URL: https://www.pramp.com/

**Interviewing.io** (Free + paid)
- What: Practice technical interviews with real interviewers
- Cost: Free tier available, $55+ for premium
- Format: Video interview, anonymous feedback
- Why: Real interviewers, recorded for playback
- URL: https://www.interviewing.io/

**Your Network** (Best option)
- Reach out to: 3-5 engineers in your network
- Ask: "Would you do a mock interview with me?"
- When: Schedule weekly for 2 weeks
- Why: Real feedback from people who know you

## 7. Path 2: Leader Resources

### Engineering Management Books (Read 2-3)

**"The Effective Manager" by Mark Horstman**
- Cost: $20-30
- Time: 8-10 hours
- Why: Practical frameworks (1-on-1s, feedback, delegation)
- Best for: New managers

**"The Manager's Path" by Camille Fournier**
- Cost: $20-30
- Time: 12-15 hours
- Why: Career progression (IC → manager → director → VP)
- Best for: Planning your trajectory

**"Radical Candor" by Kim Scott**
- Cost: $20-30
- Time: 10-12 hours
- Why: Feedback culture, caring personally + challenging directly
- Best for: Leadership style

**"An Elegant Puzzle" by Will Larson**
- Cost: $20-30
- Time: 10 hours
- Why: Technical leadership, systems thinking
- Best for: Engineering management philosophy

### Courses & Programs

   - Best for: Learning to think like a leader
